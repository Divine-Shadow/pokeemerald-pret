# Item Reward Economy

This project groups player-facing item rewards by how much they can shape a run. Use these buckets before adding field items, shop inventory, NPC gifts, or challenge rewards.

## Buckets

### Eventually Infinite

These items may appear as field rewards before the player can obtain them repeatedly. Missing one copy should not lock a build.

Examples:

- Berries
- Wild-held-item drops
- Common consumables and convenience items

Preferred sources:

- Early or midgame field items
- NPC gifts
- Later repeatable systems such as berry growth or the wild-held-item broker

Low-tier field rewards are the preferred replacement for basic potions, status heals, repels, and X items once those pickups stop feeling useful. Good low-tier examples include Pretty Feathers, one-use Gems, Sitrus/Lum/Leppa-style berries, and other small build-adjacent rewards.

### Mid-Tier Build Items

These items are meaningful rewards, but they are not the strongest build-defining tools. They can be field loot, NPC interaction rewards, or late-midgame/endgame shop stock.

Examples:

- Type boosters such as Charcoal, Magnet, Mystic Water, Miracle Seed, Soft Sand, and Black Glasses
- Accuracy, crit, and secondary build items such as Wide Lens, Scope Lens, Razor Claw, Shell Bell, Quick Claw, King's Rock, and Focus Band
- Moderate damage items such as Muscle Band, Wise Glasses, and Expert Belt

Preferred sources:

- Hidden items and optional side-path item balls
- NPC interaction rewards
- Late-midgame or endgame shops, especially when an earlier single copy already exists

### Competitive Build Items

These are strict rewards. Do not place them as ordinary field loot and do not sell them in regular shops or exchange counters. They should come only from authored optional challenges or story NPC rewards.

Current strict items:

- Choice Band
- Choice Specs
- Choice Scarf
- Eviolite
- Focus Sash
- Leftovers
- Life Orb
- Assault Vest

Focus Sash is planned as an exclusive NPC reward across multiple encounters. Until those encounters exist, keep it out of normal player acquisition.

### Pokemon-Specific Items

These items are deferred for now. Keep their current availability unless a dedicated species or form pass changes them.

Examples:

- Mega Stones
- Light Ball
- Species-specific evolution, form, or signature items

## Spoiler Policy

Broad availability rules are public. Exact field locations, shop inventories, NPC rewards, and challenge prizes are blind-friendly discovery information.

Public notes may say that mid-tier held items are available through exploration, NPCs, and late shops. Public notes must not list where a specific item is found unless the team intentionally wants that location spoiled.

## Contributor Checklist

- Classify each item before placing it.
- If an item is competitive, do not add it to normal shops, hidden items, item balls, generic exchange counters, or repeatable farming systems.
- If an item is mid-tier, prefer one interesting field or NPC copy before adding a later shop source.
- If an item is eventually infinite, it can still be a useful early field reward.
- If a basic consumable is too weak for its location, prefer a low-tier field reward over jumping straight to a mid-tier held item.
- Keep exact item locations and shop inventories in blind-friendly notes.
